The steps below will guide you through the Metadata Editor.

Please contact NKN if you have questions or if the data associated with your record are larger than 10GB.

This tool is under active development, and we welcome your feedback!

Step 1

Under Record Options in the dropdown menu below, select the type of record you wish to create (dataset vs. non-dataset). more details

Dataset records adhere to the ISO 19115 standard. Non-dataset records (e.g., tools, visualization products, models, scripts, etc.) adhere to the Dublin Core standard.

Step 2

(Optional) Load Defaults. more details

MILES users: If you are creating a record related to the MILES project, select MILES from the Load Defaults dropdown. Certain fields in the form will prepopulate with MILES default values. Please add to these fields as appropriate: for example, you will likely want to add place and thematic keywords related to your specific dataset. The bounding coordinates provided here are for the entire state of Idaho; modify to fit the location of your dataset as appropriate.

Other users: If you are using NKN to store your data, select NKN as Data Manager from the Load Defaults dropdown. Please provide the first author's name and university affiliation toward the end of the Use Restrictions text where it currently reads "(Name, University Affiliation)".

Step 3

Under Basic Information (in the form), enter the Title of your record. Then, under the Record Options dropdown, select Save this record as a draft.

NOTE: You MUST save the record as a draft in order to add/upload files to the record.

Step 4

Continue to fill out the remaining form fields, and click the Add Files button to attach documents to your record. When finished, select Save this record as a draft under the Record Options dropdown.

Step 5

To PUBLISH your record, you must complete ALL form fields (unless labeled as optional). Under the Record Options dropdown, select Publish record to NKN's portal. You will see a message stating that your Metadata Record was Submitted. more details

Once submitted, we will review and publish your record. If you make changes to a record you have already submitted, you will need to re-publish.

Tips

Use the My Records dropdown to see a list of your existing records, the date they were last modified and published, and to load a record into the webform for editing or to delete the record.

To view your metadata record as an xml document, use the View XML as... dropdown and select your preferred format (ISO, Dublin Core, or ESRI-ISO).
